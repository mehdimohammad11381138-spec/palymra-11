Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Addressing LLM "Hallucinations"

The Problem: During the enrichment phase, Large Language Models occasionally generated plausible but historically inaccurate details. For instance, one model suggested that the Temple of Bel was fully restored by 2018 using 3D-printed marble, which contradicts official reports stating that restoration is still in the planning and stabilization phases.

The Solution: A rigorous Verification Protocol was implemented. Every LLM-generated claim was cross-referenced with:

  • UNESCO World Heritage Centre reports
  • ASOR (American Society of Overseas Research) Cultural Heritage Initiatives
  • Official DGAM (Directorate-General of Antiquities and Museums) bulletins

Challenge 2: Semantic Gap in Ontologies

The Problem: Standard ontologies like CIDOC-CRM or Schema.org do not have a specific property for "Composite Authenticity." Representing the relationship between original Roman stones and modern restoration materials required more than simple existing predicates.

The Solution: We adopted a Vocabulary Extension strategy. By using rdfs:subPropertyOf, we linked our specific authenticity concepts to broader, recognized terms, ensuring both semantic precision and interoperability.

Challenge 3: Multi-Source Data Inconsistency

The Problem: Wikidata, DBpedia, and ArCo (Italian ontology) often use different IRIs for the same physical monument, leading to fragmented data when trying to build a unified Knowledge Graph.

The Solution: Implementation of owl:sameAs triples. We manually identified and linked the corresponding IRIs across different graphs to create a truly integrated linked data environment for Palmyra.
