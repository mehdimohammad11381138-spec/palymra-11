KE4H Project — Knowledge Engineering for Heritage

The Authenticity of Tourism in Palmyra A Knowledge Graph Enrichment Project

Combining Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Models to enrich cultural heritage data and explore the dimensions of authenticity in post-conflict heritage sites.

Explore Project → View Methodology

Project Overview

🏛️

Knowledge Graphs

Exploring Wikidata and DBpedia to identify gaps in cultural heritage data and enrich them with structured information about Palmyra's authenticity and restoration efforts.

🤖

LLM Integration

Leveraging multiple language models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) to extract, verify, and generate new knowledge about heritage sites, comparing their outputs and reliability.

🤝

Collaborative Approach

Individual assessment with collaborative methodology, combining SPARQL queries, prompt engineering, and RDF triple creation to build a comprehensive knowledge base.

Key Research Questions

What is Composite Authenticity?

How can we define and measure authenticity in post-conflict heritage sites that combine original materials, restoration efforts, digital documentation, and community involvement?

Can KGs and LLMs Work Together?

How effectively can structured knowledge graphs and unstructured LLM outputs be integrated to create richer, more accurate descriptions of cultural heritage?

What Data is Missing?

What specific information about Palmyra's restoration, material authenticity, and experiential value is absent from current knowledge graphs and how can it be enriched?
