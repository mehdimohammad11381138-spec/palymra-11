Conclusion

Key Findings

This KE4H project demonstrates that Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Models can be effectively combined to enrich cultural heritage data. The integration of structured (SPARQL) and unstructured (LLM) knowledge sources provides a more comprehensive understanding of complex phenomena like post-conflict heritage restoration.

The concept of "Composite Authenticity" emerges as a valuable framework for understanding heritage sites that have experienced conflict and restoration. This framework acknowledges that authenticity is multidimensional and evolves over time.

Future Directions

  • Expand to other post-conflict heritage sites globally
  • Develop automated verification systems for LLM outputs
  • Create interactive visualizations of heritage restoration timelines
  • Integrate community narratives and oral histories into the KG
  • Establish international standards for heritage authenticity assessment
